5. CREATING HIV/AIDS AWARENESS PROGRAMME


With an estimated 3.86 million people infected with HIV by 2001. The HIV/AIDS epidemic is not only the most serious public health problem in India but also one of the biggest impediments of the development goals of the country. The magnitude of the problem is not uniform through out the counter. States like U.P. are still considered as low risk with a prevalence of less than 5% among high-risk groups and less than 1% among antenatal women. In 2001, the National AIDS Control Organization (NACO) estimated that the prevalence in U.P. was 2.7% in STD patients and 0.32% among antenatal women. Creation of awareness about HIV/AIDS is the urgent need in order to control the spread of infection and prevent a full-blown epidemic from developing among peoples lives in PRATAPGARH, ALLAHABAD, RAIBARELI, KAUSHAMBHI District of U.P.

6. AWARENES CAMPAIGN ON PRE-CONCEPTON & PRE-NATAL DIAGNOSTIC TECHNIQUES (PC & PNDT ACT -1994)


According to a recent report by the United Nations Children's Fund (UNICEF) up to 5 million girls and women are missing from India's population as a result of systematic gender discrimination in India. In most countries in the world, there are approximately 15 female births for every 100 males.
In India, there are less than 93 women for every 100 men in the population. The accepted reason for such a disparity is the practice of female infanticide in India, prompted by the existence of a dowry system which requires the family to pay out a great deal of money when a female child is married. For a poor family, the birth of girl child can signal the beginning of financial ruin and extreme hardship.
