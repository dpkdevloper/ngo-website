With an estimated 3.86 million people infected with HIV by 2001. The HIV/AIDS epidemic is not only the most serious public health problem in India but also one of the biggest impediments of the development goals of the country. The magnitude of the problem is not uniform through out the counter. States like U.P. are still considered as low risk with a prevalence of less than 5% among high-risk groups and less than 1% among antenatal women. In 2001, the National AIDS Control Organization (NACO) estimated that the prevalence in U.P. was 2.7% in STD patients and 0.32% among antenatal women. Creation of awareness about HIV/AIDS is the urgent need in order to control the spread of infection and prevent a full-blown epidemic from developing among peoples lives in PRATAPGARH, ALLAHABAD, RAIBARELI, KAUSHAMBHI District of U.P. The consequences of a full blown HIV epidemic in U.P. one of the poorest states of India would be devastating. The State is in the process of recovering from the continuous onslaughts of various natural disasters like cyclone, droughts and floods. The developmental gains accrued through the processes of rehabilitation, disaster preparedness and capacity building would be nullified if the HIV/AIDS epidemic were allowed to grow to disaster proportions.



The NACO at state Level, has been functioning with the following main objectives as recommended by NACO: Targeted Interventions among High Risk Groups, STD Control, Condom Promotion, Promotion of blood safety, Establishment of Voluntary Counseling and Training Centres (VCTC) and establishing Sentinel Surveillance Centres. There are several sentinel centers to monitor the prevalence of HIV among the STD patients of PRATAPGARH, ALLAHABAD, RAIBARELI, KAUSHAMBHI district. PRATAPGARH, ALLAHABAD, RAIBARELI, KAUSHAMBHI district is one of the smaller newer western districts of bordering the state of M.P. It is largely rural drought prone, has meager resources, poor public health facilities. Low literacy and a considerable scheduled caste and scheduled tribe population. A considerable proportion of the adults are marginal workers or agricultural labourers and may of these migrants to city, the industrial towns of Punjab, Maharastra, Gujarat and Delhi during the lean- season for earning their livelihood. Thus while the risk of HIV/AIDS epidemic in PRATAPGARH, ALLAHABAD, RAIBARELI, KAUSHAMBHI district is high, as yet the infection has not spread to a significant extent and presents an opportunity of intensive control activities through the creation of awareness of PRATAPGARH, ALLAHABAD, RAIBARELI, KAUSHAMBHI District Justification: While the targeted interventions among the high-risk groups are essential, the nature of the groups targeted will determine the impact of the interventions. In PRATAPGARH, ALLAHABAD, RAIBARELI, KAUSHAMBHI among the STD patients screened for HIV 1.2% were positive in the urban areas and 3.2% in the rural areas.



This article talks about the role of posters in disseminating awareness about HIV/AIDS. India has the third largest HIV epidemic in the world, with approximately 2.1 million people living with HIV/AIDS (that is 0.2% prevalence) and 0.1% incidence, over last year. The 90 - 90 - 90 target set by the United Nations has been envisioned by India, that by end of 2020, 90% of people living with HIV (PLHIV) will know their HIV status, 90% of people who know their HIV-positive status will be accessing treatment and 90% of people on treatment will have suppressed viral loads . Achieving this is still a challenge for India, inspite of the awareness, surveillance, free drug distribution by National AIDS Control Organisation of India. There is an urgent need for health education to reduce incidence of HIV/AIDS in the future. Posters are one of the important instruments for health education amongst general population. This review will examine the utility of posters for spreading awareness about the various issues related to HIV/AIDS and the quality of life of PLHIV based on the currently available literature.
